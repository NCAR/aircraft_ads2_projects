RESULTS OF the INTERCOMPARISON FLIGHT ELECTRA/MERLIN ON 11/11/1999

Presentation

Merlin (flight 49) and ELECTRA (flight 27) carried out two leveled legs in patrol above the plain of Po. Part of the second leg was carried out under light rain. The first eight minutes of the first leg were cut off from comparison to keep only the part where both trajectories are close enough.

For each leg, the mean parameters as well as standard deviation (s ) will be compared.

Comparison of mean parameters

Temperature (cf. page 5)

Three sensors are mounted on the Electra : one on each side of the radome named ATRL and ATRR, and one - de-iced - on the fuselage named ATFH. Sensor ATRR was out of order, so ATFH is chosen as reference sensor and named ATX.

Three sensors are mounted on Merlin : Rosemount , de-iced Rosemount and Reverse flow respectively named Ts_rt_1, Ts_rd_1, Ts_rf_1. As the comparison Merlin/ARAT (flight of 29/9/1999) shows a good coherency between de-iced Rosemount and Reverse flow on one hand and two of the sensors mounted on ARAT on the other, we choose de-iced Rosemount as reference sensor for the Merlin.

So comparison was made using de-iced Rosemount of both Merlin and Electra.

Differences between these two measurements range between 0.1 and 0.2 degres.

Dew point and mixing ratio of water vapor (cf. page 6)

Two sensors named DPBC and DPTC are mounted on the Electra : one above and one under the fuselage. DPBC is chosen as reference sensor and named DPXC.

Two sensors named Td_b_sync_1 and Td_h_sync_1 are mounted on the Merlin on the rear left side of the fuselage. As the comparison Merlin/ARAT (flight of 29/9/1999) shows a very good coherency between these two sensors on one hand and those mounted on ARAT on the other, we choose the hygrometer named Td_h_sync_1 as reference sensor for the Merlin.

There is a noticeable difference, up to 4°C, between the two legs. The same difference can be found in mixing ratio of water vapor and relative humidity. Its value is up to 1.2 g/kg for mixing ratio and 16% for relative humidity.

A close examination of the temperature/dew point measurements during Electra flight nb.27 reveals that DPBC, sensor chosen as reference was not properly working. DPBC can be as high as 5°C above ATX, and the difference between DPBC and DPTC varies all flight long. DPTC seems to be good.

 

Wind speed and direction (cf. page 7)

Parameters used for the Electra are XWDC et XWSC (corrected GPS).

Parameters used for the Merlin are DD_phins_1 and FF_phins_1 computed from INS measurements.

Electra measurements seems to have more coherency but differences in speed and direction remain in the uncertainties range computed for the Merlin.

Radiation and radiative temperature (cf. pages 8,9,10)

Upward an downward visible radiation

Data values of visible radiation measurements are low. Differences range over approximately 12W for upward as for downward measurement. This confirms that, as during comparison between Merlin and ARAT, the Merlin under-estimates visible radiation of 12/15W/m2 for values below 60W.

Upward an downward infra-red radiation

Merlin and Electra measurements are very close, difference ranging under 5 W/m2. This can be used to eliminate the doubt in the comparison between Merlin and ARAT where differences range over 50 W/m2.

Radiative temperature

Only ground radiative temperatures can be compared. Electra measurements are approximately 3°C below Merlin measurements. In the comparison between Merlin and ARAT, Merlin measurements were approximately less than 1.5°C below ARAT measurements.

 

 

 

Conclusion

  1. Temperature : use reference ATX for the Electra and Ts_rd_1 for the Merlin.

  2. Dew point : use DPTC as reference and not DPBC (a 1°C difference will remain between Electra and Merlin).

  3. Wind : the choice between wind measurements made by the Electra remains dubious. Electra data computed from corrected GPS and Merlin data computed from INS are closer.

  4. Radiation :

    1. Upward and downward visible : Merlin under-estimates of 12/15 W/m2

    2. Upward and downward infra-red : very close

    3. downward Barnes : ARAT> Merlin> Electra
